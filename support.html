How to stay safe after reporting:

  • Document Everything: Keep detailed records of incidents, including dates, times, and witnesses. This documentation is crucial for your case.
  • Follow Internal Grievance Procedures: Report the harassment through your employer’s internal grievance procedure. Ensure your complaint is taken seriously and documented.
  • Know Your Rights: Understand your rights under South African labor laws, which include protection against retaliation and the right to a safe workplace.
  • Escalate if Necessary: If your employer fails to address the issue, you can escalate your complaint to the Commission for Conciliation, Mediation and Arbitration (CCMA) or Labour Court.
  • Seek Legal Advice: Consult with labor law attorneys to understand your rights and the legal options available to you. 1 By following these steps, you can ensure that your safety and rights are protected after reporting a labor harassment case.

What counts as harassment:

  • Verbal Harassment: This includes offensive jokes, slurs, name-calling, or any derogatory comments directed at an individual based on their protected characteristics, such as race, gender, or sexual orientation.
  • Physical Harassment: This can involve unwanted physical contact, threats of violence, or intimidation through physical presence.
  • Online Harassment: Also known as cyberbullying, this includes sending threatening or abusive messages through social media, emails, or other online platforms.
  • Sexual Harassment: This is a specific type of harassment that involves unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature.
  • Conclusion Understanding what constitutes harassment is crucial for both victims seeking protection and individuals ensuring their behavior does not cross legal boundaries. If you believe you are experiencing harassment, it may be beneficial to document the behavior and seek legal advice to understand your rights and options for recourse.

How to document incidents:

  • Keep a Detailed Log: Record Each Incident: Write down the date, time, and location of each incident. Include the names and titles of everyone involved, including witnesses. 2 Describe the Incident: Provide a detailed description of what happened, including specific words or actions. Use direct quotes if possible to capture the exact language used.
  • Gather Evidence: Save Physical Evidence: Keep any physical items related to the harassment, such as inappropriate notes or gifts. Take photos of any evidence, including injuries or offensive materials. 2 Document Digital Evidence: Save emails, text messages, or any other digital communications that demonstrate harassment. Take screenshots if necessary.
  • Identify Witnesses: Note Witness Information: If there were witnesses to the harassment, document their names and contact information. Include a brief description of what they observed.
  • Follow Company Policies: Report the Incident: After documenting the harassment, report it according to your company’s policies. Provide your documentation to the appropriate person or department, such as HR. 2 Consult Your Employee Handbook: Review your company’s anti-harassment policies to understand the procedures for reporting incidents.
  • Keep Everything Organized: Secure Your Documentation: Store your records in a safe place, ensuring that they are accessible only to those involved in the investigation. 1 Maintain a Daily Log: Use a notebook or digital app to keep track of incidents as they occur, ensuring that you capture details while they are fresh in your mind.

Which evidence to keep:

  • Internal Grievance Procedures: Document any formal complaints lodged through your employer's internal grievance procedure, including details of the incident and the response provided.
  • Code of Good Practice: Review the Code of Good Practice on the Prevention and Elimination of Harassment, which outlines the legal obligations for employers and provides a framework for addressing harassment.
  • Documentation of Incidents: Maintain records of all incidents of harassment, including dates, descriptions, and any relevant witnesses or documentation.
  • Training and Awareness: Keep records of any training sessions or awareness programs conducted regarding harassment policies and procedures.
  • These steps can help ensure that evidence of workplace harassment is properly documented and maintained for legal and organizational purposes.

Here are tips to help with coping:

  • Seek Support: Consider reaching out to coworkers you feel safe around, or talk to a therapist for additional support. Journaling can also help you process your feelings and experiences.
  • Know Your Rights: Understand your rights to work in a harassment-free environment. Reporting is a way to assert your rights and seek justice.
  • Stay Informed: Keep up with your company's policies and procedures regarding harassment, and be aware of any updates or changes that may affect your situation.
  • These steps can help you navigate the aftermath of reporting harassment and support your recovery process.
